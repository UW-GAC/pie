About

TOPMed

TOPMed is the Trans-Omics for Precision Medicine project, sponsored by the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute (NHLBI). TOPMed's whole genome sequencing project, started in 2014, will integrate rich phenotype data with whole genome sequences for tens of thousands of subjects to further characterize the genetic architecture of heart, lung, blood, and sleep disorders.

The TOPMed Data Coordinating Center (DCC)

The Data Coordinating Center is based in the Biostatistics Department at the University of Washington and is led by Ken Rice (contact PI), Bruce Weir (co-PI), Bruce Psaty (co-PI) and Cathy Laurie.

The DCC provides administrative and logistical support, organizes phenotype Working Groups, coordinates genome sequencing centers, and develops guidelines and methodology for genetic analyses.

TOPMed Phenotype Harmonization

Harmonization of the source phenotype data is being completed at the TOPMed DCC.
